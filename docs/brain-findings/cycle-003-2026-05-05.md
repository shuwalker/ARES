## HASEL Actuator Deep-Dive — RES-004 Progress

### Key Paper 1: Anthropomorphic Musculoskeletal Hand (ETH Zurich, March 2026)
**arXiv:2603.24357** — Sonoda, Hinchet, Kazemipour, Toshimitsu, Katzschmann

This is the most practically relevant HASEL paper for JP01. Key findings:
- **Peano-HASEL actuators** placed in forearm (remote actuation via tendons)
- **1:2 pulley routing** mechanically amplifies displacement (addresses limited linear contraction)
- **Self-sensing via current monitoring** — no external force sensors needed for grasp detection
- **Inherently compliant and force-limiting** — can grasp fragile objects (paper balloons) without damage
- **Sensorless closed-loop control** from actuator current alone
- No encoders, no force transducers

- HASELs exhibit **memory effects and hysteresis** — nonlinear multiphysics dynamics

### HASEL vs BLDC+FOC for JP01

HASELs are NOT a drop-in replacement for BLDC+FOC servos. They're a fundamentally different actuation paradigm:
- HASEL = inherently silent, compliant, low-force, short-stroke, high-voltage, exotic materials
- BLDC+FOC = quiet-when-done-right, stiff, high-force, long-stroke, low-voltage, off-the-shelf

**Recommendation:** HASELs could complement JP01's BLDC+FOC backbone for specific quiet joints (face, small expressive elements) but should NOT be the primary actuation technology. The BLDC+FOC + cable-drive approach validated in the QDD hand paper (2405.18730) remains the practical path for JP01's main joints.
